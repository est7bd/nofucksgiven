The Dollar Slipping and Oil Moving to Yen Should Scare the Hell Out of You

The U.S. dollar losing ground while oil starts getting traded in yen is not some abstract financial headline you can scroll past. It's a warning shot. And the fact that most people are either ignoring it or actively downplaying it tells you everything about how far gone the discourse is.

This Is Bigger Than "Markets Fluctuate"

For decades — decades — the dollar has been the default. Oil? Dollars. Global trade? Dollars. International power? Quietly backed by the assumption that everyone needs dollars.

That's not just an economic fact. That's the foundation. And when you start seeing that shift — even a little, even at the margins — that's not noise. That's the foundation cracking.

Yeah, That's a Big Fucking Problem

When oil starts moving outside dollar-denominated systems, it chips away at the one thing that's quietly held U.S. economic dominance together longer than most people have been alive.

You don't lose that overnight. But you also don't ignore the early signs unless you're stupid, willfully blind, or politically incentivized to pretend everything is fine when it isn't.

Guess which one describes most of what we're hearing right now.

This Is What Decline Actually Looks Like

Not some dramatic single collapse. Not a moment you can point to. Small shifts. Quiet changes. Things that seem manageable right up until they're not. By the time it's obvious to everyone, you've already lost the window to respond.

Nobody Wants to Say It Out Loud

Because saying it means admitting the U.S. isn't untouchable. That global systems are reorganizing around new realities. That we're not automatically at the center of everything anymore.

That's deeply uncomfortable for a country that's spent 80 years acting like its dominance was a law of nature instead of a circumstance. So people downplay it. They call it temporary. They change the subject.

Bottom Line

If the dollar stops being the world's default currency, everything changes. Not overnight. But inevitably and irreversibly.

And the fact that we're already watching the early signs of that shift — while the people responsible are busy posting on Truth Social and picking fights with the Vatican — should scare the shit out of you.

It scares me.
